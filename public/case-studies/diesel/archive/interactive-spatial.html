Executive Summary

Diesel Iceberg is a flagship concept that bans phones on the retail floors to manufacture the rarest thing in luxury retail: an opinion you formed yourself. Built in Harajuku under the Glenn Martens era, it forces digital disconnection so the body and the mirrorless room do the work the feed used to do.

Ten percent product. Ninety percent the kind of soul that sells product. Entry trades your phone for a solid milled brass token stamped For Successful Living 1978: the only object in the building without a price tag. The apex is Ghiaccio, where phones return and the story earns its post.

01Context

Self-expression became self-performance. Fashion followed.

In the mid-2020s, getting dressed became a content production decision. The algorithm didn't kill personal style: it monetized it, rewarding looks that were just edgy enough to catch the eye but safe enough to earn validation. Diesel, under Glenn Martens, had rebuilt its cultural credibility globally. Its retail stores had not kept pace.

02Insight

Diesel's real enemy isn't conformity. It's fake rebellion.

True non-conformity requires risk: the real possibility of being misunderstood by the people physically in the room. The algorithm eliminated that risk entirely. It replaced it with a pre-approved version of weirdness: curated, optimized, waiting for likes to confirm the outfit worked. People were no longer dressing for themselves. They were dressing for an audience that wasn't even present.

“Find out who you are first, post later.”

03Idea

The store physically removes your ability to perform.

Diesel Iceberg is a five-floor flagship in Harajuku (historically Tokyo's permission zone for authentic self-expression before the feed colonized it) built entirely around forced digital disconnection. Entry requires surrendering your phone. In return: a solid milled brass token, stamped For Successful Living 1978. The only item in the building without a price tag.

05Scale
05, Scale
Export the blueprint

The blueprint is designed to be exported, not replicated.

Harajuku was chosen as proof-of-concept for the world's most discerning intrinsic luxury buyers. Japanese luxury consumers purchase for personal satisfaction, not status signaling: the hardest audience to fake. Success here unlocks the broader Asian market. London and Shanghai are next.

The psychological and architectural framework scales; the experience doesn't need to be identical to carry the same argument.

06BBrand in the World

01 · Store Fixtures

The Iceberg communicates through material restraint. Volcanic stone cash wraps, milled steel rails, and raw concrete surfaces. No softness, no padding: every surface is a deliberate argument for the body over the brand.

Fixtures: Volcanic Stone & Steel.
Signage: Brutalist Typography.
Architecture: Raw Concrete & Kintsugi Joinery.

02 · Packaging System

Material constants across every touchpoint. The brass token stamped with the founding ethos. Vacuum-sealed lab kits for Denim Lab artifacts. Every object you leave with carries the same argument as the building you just exited.

Packaging: Vacuum-Sealed Lab Kits.
Token: For Successful Living · 1978.

03 · Employee Style Guide

The team is the first signal. Tonal denim utility: nothing precious, nothing performative. Staff dress the way the building argues you should: for yourself, not for the photo. The stylist relationship is the highest-trust asset in the building.

Style Guide: Tonal Denim Utility: dressed for the building, not the camera.
07Legacy
07 (Legacy
Conditions for opinion

Diesel stopped selling clothes and started selling the conditions for a genuine opinion.

Authenticity became the luxury good) not because the word tested well in research, but because it had become genuinely scarce. Diesel Iceberg is the first flagship built on the premise that removing a tool from a customer's hand is the most valuable service a brand can provide.

The brands that survive the next decade of digital saturation won't be the ones with the most visibility. They'll be the ones that gave people somewhere to think.
